# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Staff at a tree service company (single-tenant, no RBAC — every authenticated user has equal access). They build quotes in the field or office, often offline, on phones and laptops. Secondary audience: the company's clients, who view/approve quotes and invoices through an unauthenticated public portal link (no account, no app knowledge assumed).

## Product Purpose

An offline-first quote/estimate builder: draft a priced quote with photos, sync it when connectivity returns, send it to the client for approval, track it through scheduling to a completed invoice. Success = staff can build and send a quote reliably with zero data loss, and the client-facing portal/emails read as credible and professional enough to win the job.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, solid, professional. This is a tree service company — heavy outdoor labor, real money quoted to real homeowners. The interface should read like an established, competent business, not a flashy startup. Confidence through clarity and restraint, not decoration for its own sake.

## Anti-references

- Not generic SaaS-cliché: no purple/blue gradients, no stock "AI-generated template" look.
- Not playful/childish: this is heavy labor and real invoices — avoid bright toy-like colors, cutesy icons, or overly rounded/bubbly UI.

## Design Principles

1. Clarity over decoration — every visual flourish must earn its place; staff use this daily under real work pressure (sometimes outdoors, sometimes mid-job).
2. Credibility for the client-facing surfaces — the portal, emails, and invoice PDF are the client's main impression of the company; they must look like a real, professional business.
3. Calm confidence, not hype — solid neutral palette + one deliberate accent, not a rainbow of gradients or attention-grabbing motion.
4. Motion as polish, not spectacle — transitions should feel smooth and intentional (state changes, saves, syncs), never bouncy or distracting.
5. Offline/sync states must stay legible — status badges and sync indicators are functional information, not just decoration; never sacrifice their clarity for style.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, visible focus states. No special outdoor/high-contrast mode requested beyond AA compliance. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
